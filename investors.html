PlantOn

Vision of founders

PlantOn is building the scalable, software-defined food production network in Europe.

We are transforming food from a fragmented, inefficient supply chain into a distributed, digitally controlled infrastructure layer — where standardized production units (“nodes”) are deployed across geographies and orchestrated by a single platform.

This is not farming.
This is infrastructure for food.

The Problem

Europe’s food system is structurally broken:

  • Supply chains are long, opaque, and fragile
  • Restaurants face unreliable sourcing and price volatility
  • Consumers lack transparency, trust, and control
  • Small producers carry disproportionate risk with minimal upside

Current solutions optimize logistics or marketplaces — but nobody owns production + demand + software as one system.

The PlantOn Solution

PlantOn vertically integrates:

  • Standardized local production nodes
  • Protected, controlled cultivation
  • Real-time digital oversight
  • Dual-market distribution
  • Platform-level orchestration

Each node is a self-contained production unit:

  • Rapid deployment
  • Predictable output
  • Digitally monitored
  • Centrally managed
  • Locally operated

The platform is the control layer:

  • Production
  • Allocation
  • Orders
  • Distribution
  • Performance analytics
  • Expansion logic

This creates a replicable infrastructure model, not a farming operation.

Business Model

PlantOn operates a dual-engine revenue system:

B2C Engine – Subscriptions

  • Consumers subscribe to dedicated production capacity
  • Prepaid revenue
  • High retention
  • Emotional ownership model
  • Predictable cash flow

B2B Engine – Restaurants

  • Weekly contracted supply
  • Volume stability
  • Premium pricing for reliability
  • Long-term relationships
  • Low acquisition cost

Each node is split 50/50 B2C and B2B, balancing: Margin, Volume, Risk, Cash flow stability.

Technology Platform

PlantOn is not an app — it is an operating system for distributed food production.

The platform enables:

  • Node management
  • Production planning
  • Crop allocation
  • Customer management
  • Restaurant procurement
  • Logistics coordination
  • Performance analytics
  • Multi-country operations

This creates data gravity, network effects, and operational defensibility.

Validation Strategy

The initial pilot in Slavonia, Croatia is a controlled validation environment:

  • Standardized node deployment
  • Active B2C subscriptions
  • Contracted restaurant demand
  • Live production
  • Live deliveries
  • Live platform usage

The objective is not proof of concept —
the objective is proof of repeatability.

Scaling Strategy

Following validation, PlantOn will deploy:

  • 4–5 nodes per country
  • Across Croatia, Italy, Portugal, and the UK
  • 16–20 nodes total in first expansion phase

Each node is: CapEx-efficient · Rapidly deployable · Operationally standardized · Platform-managed

Scaling is not linear farming growth — It is infrastructure replication.

Market Opportunity

PlantOn sits at the intersection of:

  • AgriTech
  • Climatech
  • Food Infrastructure
  • Subscription Economy

This is a massive, structurally broken market with no dominant digital infrastructure layer.

PlantOn is building that layer.
